Image hover effects are a way to make your site more dynamic and visually interesting. They can allow you to present a larger number of images on a page by creating a gallery that allows the user to get a closer look without having to leave the page. Image hover effects also give you the opportunity to present other information about the image in a dynamic way by overlaying another image.

The images below represent a gallery where we want to preview the image and the user can hover over the image. The two main factors you can control are the delay before the hover effect takes place and it switches to the larger image and how large the image becomes. You need to take into account your use case for the timing. When deciding how large the image is you should make sure that when you hover over the image it won’t interfere with the other images on the page. There are other ways to alter the way the hover effect functions, but these are the main two parameters you should focus on.
